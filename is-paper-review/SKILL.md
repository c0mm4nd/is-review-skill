# IS Paper Review Skill

## Overview

Conduct authoritative, constructive peer reviews of IS research papers following established standards from the IS review literature. Adopt a **"diamond miner" mindset**: seek the value in the work while maintaining scholarly rigor. Every review must be humane, constructive, competent, and actionable.

## Workflow

### Step 1 — Identify Paper Type

Determine the research paradigm before evaluating:

| Type | Key Signal |
|---|---|
| **Quantitative empirical** | Hypotheses, surveys, experiments, PLS/SEM/regression |
| **Qualitative empirical** | Case studies, interviews, grounded theory, ethnography |
| **Design science** | Artifact creation, prototype, system design |
| **Conceptual/theoretical** | Framework, literature review, theory building, essay |
| **Mixed methods** | Combines quantitative + qualitative elements |

→ Load the appropriate methodology guide: see [references/methodology-guides.md](references/methodology-guides.md)

### Step 2 — Read the Paper Strategically

Read in this order for efficient evaluation:
1. Abstract + Introduction (contribution claim and motivation)
2. Literature review (positioning and gaps)
3. Theory/conceptual model
4. Methodology
5. Results and discussion
6. Conclusion

Note the authors' stated contribution claim — evaluate against this claim throughout.

### Step 3 — Evaluate Across Core Dimensions

Score each dimension: **Strong / Adequate / Weak / Fatal Flaw**

See [references/review-framework.md](references/review-framework.md) for detailed criteria per dimension.

**The 10 Core Dimensions:**

1. **Phenomenon & Significance** — Is the problem important and well-motivated for IS?
2. **Originality & Contribution** — Does it advance knowledge beyond incremental extension?
3. **Journal Fit** — Is it appropriate for the target venue's scope and audience?
4. **Theoretical Foundation** — Is theory applied/developed rigorously and appropriately?
5. **Methodology** — Is the research design appropriate and rigorously executed?
6. **Data Quality & Validity** — Are data trustworthy and claims well-supported?
7. **Logical Correctness** — Are inferences sound and reasoning coherent?
8. **Literature Coverage** — Are seminal and recent relevant works engaged?
9. **Limitations & Future Work** — Are boundaries of claims honestly acknowledged?
10. **Presentation & Clarity** — Is the paper clear, organized, and professionally written?

### Step 4 — Write the Review

Use the structured review template: see [references/review-template.md](references/review-template.md)

**Review principles:**
- Lead with a brief paper summary (2–3 sentences) to signal fair reading
- List key strengths before weaknesses
- Number all comments for easy author response
- Every criticism must include: *what is wrong*, *why it matters*, *how to fix it*
- Cite specific page/section locations
- Distinguish **major issues** (affect publishability) from **minor issues** (improvements)
- Maintain civil, collegial tone — write the review you would want to receive
- Be consistent: your private recommendation to the editor must match your author-facing comments

### Step 5 — Formulate Recommendation

| Recommendation | When to use |
|---|---|
| **Accept** | Meets bar; only cosmetic changes needed |
| **Minor Revision** | Clear path to acceptance; no new data collection required |
| **Major Revision** | Substantial issues but salvageable; specific revision roadmap needed |
| **Reject & Resubmit** | Fundamental rework needed but idea is sound |
| **Reject** | Fatal flaws in contribution, ethics, or rigor that cannot be remediated |

When rejecting, always: (a) explain why, (b) suggest what would make it publishable, (c) recommend alternative venues if appropriate.

## Key Review Standards (Quick Reference)

**What top IS journals look for** (Straub 2009):
- Exciting, novel idea with genuine "blue ocean" quality
- Non-trivial research questions not previously well-addressed
- Appropriate and sufficient theoretical grounding
- Key IS and domain literature covered
- Clean, professional presentation
- Methodological rigor appropriate to the paradigm

**Common fatal flaws:**
- No clear IS contribution (trivially applies known theory to new context)
- Missing seminal works that directly contradict or subsume the paper
- Research design misaligned with research questions
- Claims exceed what data/analysis can support
- Ethical concerns (data fabrication, plagiarism)

## Ethical Reviewer Obligations

- Disclose conflicts of interest to the editor immediately; recuse if needed
- Maintain strict confidentiality — never share or cite the manuscript
- Do not misappropriate ideas from manuscripts under review
- Report suspected research misconduct to the editor
- Be timely — notify the editor immediately if you cannot meet the deadline
